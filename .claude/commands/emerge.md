Surface what my vault implies but I have never actually said. Focus (optional):
$ARGUMENTS

## Vault access

Prefer the Obsidian CLI (see the `obsidian-cli` skill); fall back to
Glob/Grep/Read over the vault folder if Obsidian isn't running. If no vault
can be located, ask me for the path first.

## What to scan

- The last 60–90 days of daily notes.
- Graph structure: `obsidian orphans` (isolated thinking), `obsidian
  unresolved` (links to notes that don't exist yet — named but never written),
  `obsidian tags counts sort=count` (where attention actually pools).
- The most-linked and most-recently-touched permanent notes.

## What to look for

Three distinct kinds of latency:

1. **Conclusions from scattered premises** — I've written A in one note, B in
   another, C in a third; together they entail D, but D appears nowhere.
2. **Unnamed patterns** — a structure I keep circling across domains or months
   without ever naming it. (Repeated unresolved links and recurring phrasing
   are strong signals.)
3. **Unarticulated directions** — where several active threads are converging:
   a project, move, or position the vault is clearly walking toward but has
   never stated as an intention.
4. **Clusters ready to graduate** — groups of related notes that have quietly
   reached critical mass and could become a project, essay, or product.

## Output

3–7 findings, strongest first. For each:

- **The naming** — the implied idea stated plainly in one sentence, as if
  adding the missing note's first line.
- **The premises** — the scattered evidence: files, dates, short quotes, and
  the notes that connect to it.
- **Why it stayed latent** — one sentence (spread across domains, adjacent to
  something uncomfortable, vocabulary didn't exist yet...).
- **What naming it unlocks** — what becomes thinkable or doable once stated.
  If the finding is a cluster, say what it's ready to become (project, essay,
  product) and what the first concrete step would be.

Quality bar: each finding should be something I have *not* said. If I've
already written the conclusion somewhere, it doesn't belong here. Fewer, truer
findings beat a long list.

**Read-only**: never create, edit, or delete vault files. If a finding
deserves to become a note, I'll write it myself — that's the point.
