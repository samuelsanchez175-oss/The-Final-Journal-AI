Show me my drift: the gap between where I said I was going and where my
attention actually went. Window (optional): $ARGUMENTS

## Vault access

Prefer the Obsidian CLI (see the `obsidian-cli` skill); fall back to
Glob/Grep/Read over the vault folder if Obsidian isn't running. If no vault
can be located, ask me for the path first.

## Gather two records

1. **Stated intentions** — from 30–60 days back: goals, priorities, plans,
   and commitments. Search for intention language ("I want to", "I should",
   "this month", "focus", "priority", "next step") in daily notes, project
   context files, and planning notes from the start of the window.
2. **Actual behavior** — what the daily notes from then until now show I
   *actually* worked on, wrote about, and gave attention to. Tag counts and
   link activity (`obsidian tags counts sort=count`, recently modified files)
   are useful proxies for where attention pooled.

## Diff them

- **Held** — intentions I stated and actually followed through on.
- **Drifted from** — intentions that quietly stalled: for each, the stated
  intention (quoted, dated) vs. what actually happened (evidence from daily
  notes). No judgment in the description — just the two records side by side.
- **Avoiding** — the strongest signal: things I keep *re-stating* but never
  acting on, or visibly steer around (mentioned then dropped, repeatedly
  postponed, always "next week"). Name each one plainly.
- **Drifting toward** — the other side of drift: recurring themes or phrases
  appearing across unrelated notes that I never declared as an intention.
  What am I moving toward without realizing it? This is often the real
  finding — drift isn't always failure; sometimes it's an undeclared
  priority asserting itself.

## Close

End with one honest question per major drift, phrased so I could journal on
it tonight: is this drift a failure of follow-through to correct, or a signal
that the stated intention was wrong and should be rewritten?

**Read-only**: never create, edit, or delete vault files.
